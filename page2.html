I'm a daughter

I am the youngest daughter in our family, with a seven-year gap between me and my older sister. My parents hadn’t planned on having me, but life has a way of surprising us. Despite our busy lives, we always make it a point to spend quality time together on weekends, keeping our family bond strong. One thing I truly appreciate is that my father never made us feel inadequate or expressed a desire for a son. With three daughters, I believe our home was already complete in his eyes. My mother shares that sentiment and often reminds us with her favorite saying: "You will never regret having children, but you might regret not having them."

I'm a sister

I’ve got two older sisters, and honestly, I’m pretty lucky to have them around. They have paved the way for me, making my journey a little smoother. Whenever I encounter difficulties, I remind myself of their experiences—if they were able to overcome such challenges, I know I can as well. The struggles I face now are ones they have already conquered. They inspire me to be stronger than my fears and remind me that everything is a learning experience, and in time, all challenges will pass. Although we may have our disagreements, I am certain of the love we share, even if we express it differently.

I'm a cousin

I’m lucky to have a wonderful bunch of girl cousins on my mom’s side! With a big age gap between me and my sisters, I find it so easy to connect with my younger cousins over shared interests and all the fun things in life. Even though I’m the youngest daughter, I get to step into the role of an older sister, someone they can look up to. We have such a blast together—no drama, just pure enjoyment! They’re the built-in friends I never knew I needed, and I feel so privileged to have them in my life.
